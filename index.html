My name is Hoang. I was born and raised in Vietnam. Throughout my life and academic journey, I've been someone who loves to explore, and take initiatives and risks while remaining disciplined with my decision making. Specifically, during my high school years, I was exposed to knowledge about markets, businesses, and technology, which is when I started to build out my ECAs portfolio to pursue studying abroad in the US, which is a risky idea to be thinking about for Asian parents. I was attracted to Northeastern because of their signature Co-Op program and the entrepreneurial spirit that stems from it. When I came to Northeastern, I always knew I wanted to pursue finance but was not sure what space I’m really passionate in, which is why I began to narrow down my interests by participating in NU PE/VC, and internship experience at Dragon Capital, a public equity asset management firm, VinaCapital Ventures, a venture capital branch of an investment fund, and recently for my first co-op at Brown Brothers Harriman’s fund accounting division. Through my experiences across different areas of finance, I’ve learned that I genuinely enjoy the analytical intensity of underwriting risks.

Outside of school, I enjoy exploring different hobbies and learning new things. I like photography, traveling, and trying new food. Photography allows me to capture interesting moments and beautiful places, while traveling helps me experience different cultures and perspectives.
